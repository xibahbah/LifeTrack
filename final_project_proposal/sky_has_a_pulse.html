The Sky
Has a Pulse

A living migration archive built from white stork accelerometers. Each glow is not a location. It is a body in motion.
01 / Wake the archive

Migration begins as a field of quiet organisms.

Scroll and the sky changes scale. The flock is not summarized; it is encountered.

02 / Scrub time

A month becomes a beam of attention.

The range control moves through the sensor record. Pulses brighten when a bird's body becomes more active.

03 / Touch a pulse

Hovering becomes observation.

Nearby pulses lean toward the cursor. Identity appears only when the reader gets close.

04 / Enter one life

Click a bird and the ecosystem folds into an individual rhythm.

The small bars show a monthly pulse signature: gaps, bursts, and long stretches of signal.

05 / Feel disappearance

Some rhythms fracture into red.

Outcome is not a filter menu. It becomes atmosphere: the archive carries survival and loss at the same time.
